Chapter 180: Chapter 180: The Nightmare Has Ended

During this period, Kong Shengbin took the medication twice more. In between, his condition only flared up once, and its severity was much lighter than before.

This news was a tremendous morale booster for the medical staff in the quarantine zone. Instead of keeping it hidden, Zhu Tianlei had Li Ping inform the patients through the nurses.

With something to look forward to, everyone seemed wound up like clockwork toys, tirelessly helping Zhu Tianlei prepare treatment drugs.

A week later, the conditions of all patients in the quarantine zone stabilized. Kong Shengbin, who was one of the first to take the medication, never experienced another relapse.

Through examination, Zhu Tianlei found that Kong Shengbin's body had fully recovered to normal.

Since then, Kong Shengbin, who had been the first person to fall ill, also became the first to recover.

Ten days later, Zhu Tianlei compiled the treatment results into a report and sent it to Tang Wen. Thinking about such a severe epidemic causing so many deaths being completely cured, Zhu Tianlei's hand trembled slightly as he pressed the send button.

In another ten days, if there were no relapses in the patients' conditions and the test results met the standards, they could be evacuated from the quarantine zone and the disease alert could be lifted.

Ten days later, all patients' health examination reports were sent to the Yunhai City Health Bureau. When Director Ren Congzhou of the Health Bureau stood outside the iron gate of the quarantine zone and announced that everyone could be evacuated, a sea of sobs erupted within.

Those who had never experienced it would not know that kind of terror and despair; they naturally couldn't feel the excitement and joy of seeing sunlight once more at that moment.

Zhu Tianlei stood at the back of the crowd, watching with a faint smile as patients were embraced and kissed by their relatives. He watched his fellow medical staff members, who had fought alongside him, smiling through tears. Finally, he let out a long sigh.

"Thank you, Doctor Zhu! It's Doctor Zhu who saved our lives; Doctor Zhu is our lifesaver!"

No one knew who said it first, but everyone turned and bowed deeply in Zhu Tianlei's direction. Even those relatives who had not experienced this ordeal shed tears while bowing.

Inside and outside the quarantine zone, a sea of people bowed to the same person.

It was a sign of respect for life and humanity.

Zhu Tianlei also bent down and returned their bows. While they thanked him, he felt gratitude toward them as well; in just one month, it seemed he had gained new insights into himself and life.

No one knew who said it first, but everyone turned and bowed deeply in Zhu Tianlei's direction. Even those relatives who had not experienced the ordeal shed tears while bowing.

Inside and outside the quarantine zone, a sea of people bowed to the same person.

It was a profound sign of respect for life and humanity.

Zhu Tianlei also bent down and returned their bows. While they thanked him, he felt immense gratitude toward them as well; in just one month, it seemed he had gained new perspectives on himself and life.

When everyone slowly dispersed, several graceful figures appeared in Zhu Tianlei's line of sight.

Four women, each holding a large bouquet of fresh flowers, stood in the sunlight, smiling at her.

Behind them, Mao Yaoyi with her pretty short hair smiled radiantly as well.

Their gazes met, and each person’s lips slowly curled upward. After surviving such trauma, they could only embrace beauty for the rest of their lives.

At the women's insistence, Zhu Tianlei took a bath, changed clothes, crossed the fire basin, and was even forced to wear a pair of red panties.

Returning to her home which she had left for a month, Zhu Tianlei suddenly felt his eyes welling up with tears.

Zhu Quezi and Hou Suqin said nothing, but from their obviously grayed hair, Zhu Tianlei could imagine the torment they had endured over the past month.

After dinner.

As usual, Zhu Quezi turned on the television. Watching evening news was a habit Zhu Quezi had developed over the years.

Zhu Tianlei stretched his arms and planned to go out for a walk. Having been confined in the isolation zone for a month, he needed to find an opportunity to be close to his woman.

But before he could leave, he heard the sound of the television.

"President Tang, you have developed this special drug to treat 'Deng Luolin Virus,' which can be considered a great benefit to humanity. Could you please tell us about the process of developing this drug?"

"You are too kind. As a medical worker, my responsibility is to heal the sick and injured. As long as I can alleviate the patients' suffering and contribute to the country, it is my honor. Although the process was lengthy, it was necessary and not worth mentioning."

On the screen, Tang Wen wore a silver-gray Zhongshan suit, his hair slightly gray, wearing gold-rimmed glasses, smiling gracefully.

Zhu Tianlei clenched his fists.

"Tianlei, what’s going on here? The drug was clearly developed by you, how did it become Tang Wen's achievement?"

Hao Xinmei's phone call came almost immediately after the news was broadcast.

"I don't know either," Zhu Tianlei said truthfully. He couldn't figure out what Tang Wen was up to now. In his memory, Tang Wen had always been a very competent dean, and he had praised Tang Wen in front of Yang Wenhua more than once.

"Wait here, I'll be right over!" Hao Xinmei hung up the phone angrily. Why should she, Hao Xinmei, be treated as someone to bully?

Mao Yu slammed the folder on the table with a bang and rushed out of the office seconds later.

"Tianlei, did you see the news? What's going on here? How could the medicine become Vice Dean Tang's project?"

Li Ping and Li Yong's phones rang one after another.

Tang Wen had always been popular in the hospital. Compared to Wei Baoguo, who was rigid and looked down on everyone, Tang Wen was a nice guy, and most of the staff respected him. Therefore, when this incident happened, Li Ping's tone carried a hint of disbelief.

Less than two hours later, Zhu's courtyard became bustling with activity.

Not only did several women arrive, but Song Binián and Yang Hua also rushed over after hearing about it.

When the health bureau issued the call for medical aid, Yang Hua had signed up right away, but her application was rejected due to her age.

However, both Song Binián and Yang Hua had never lost their interest in Rui Tianlei's isolation zone research. Therefore, they knew about Tianlei's discovery of a cure for infectious diseases.

"Tianlei, what's going on?"

"President Song, to be frank, I don't know either, but it doesn't matter to me."

Zhu Tianlei's statement wasn't mere politeness; he didn't need anything to bolster or brag about himself. Moreover, he always believed that fate was fair, and taking what wasn't his would eventually come with a price.

Mao Yü was so enraged that steam seemed to be coming out of her ears, her face filled with fury.
